# Tank Setup

## Tank Size

Turtles need enough horizontal swimming space to move naturally. A larger water volume is easier to keep clean and stable than a very small tank.

## Water Depth

Water depth should match the turtle's species, size, and swimming ability. Deep water should include ramps, resting ledges, or platforms so the turtle can reach the surface without exhaustion.

## Dry Basking Area

Aquatic and semi-aquatic turtles need a completely dry basking area. The turtle should be able to leave the water fully, dry its shell, and sit under UVB and heat lighting.

## Ramps And Resting Areas

Ramps should be stable, non-slip, and easy to climb. Resting areas are especially important for young turtles, weak swimmers, and species that prefer shallower setups.

## Substrate And Decorations

Avoid small gravel that may be swallowed. Avoid sharp decorations and unstable rock piles. Any decoration should be easy to clean and should not trap the turtle underwater.

## Escape Prevention

Turtles can climb surprisingly well. The tank should prevent escape while still allowing safe lighting placement and ventilation.
