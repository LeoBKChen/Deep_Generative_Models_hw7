# Common Health Issues

TurtleCare AI does not provide veterinary diagnosis.
If symptoms are severe, persistent, or rapidly worsening, consult a qualified reptile veterinarian.

## Refusing Food

Refusing food may be related to stress, temperature, water quality, seasonal behavior, illness, or unsuitable diet. Check husbandry first, but seek professional help if refusal continues.

## Swollen Eyes

Swollen or closed eyes may be associated with water quality, irritation, nutrition issues, infection, or other causes. Do not assume one definite cause.

## Floating Abnormally

Abnormal floating, imbalance, or inability to dive can be serious. It may relate to buoyancy problems, respiratory issues, weakness, or other conditions.

## Shell Softness Or Damage

Soft shell, severe shell damage, or unusual shell odor requires careful attention. Check UVB, calcium, basking access, and water quality, and consult a reptile veterinarian when needed.

## Skin Lesions And Wounds

Wounds, bleeding, or skin lesions may become infected. Separate aggressive tank mates and seek professional care for serious injuries.

## Breathing Difficulty

Noisy breathing, open-mouth breathing, bubbles from the nose, or severe lethargy can be warning signs. Consult a qualified reptile veterinarian promptly.
