# Mixed Species Warning

## General Risk

Mixed-species turtle housing is risky. Different species may have different sizes, diets, temperatures, swimming ability, and behavior.

## Size Differences

Large turtles may injure smaller turtles, even without obvious aggression. Bites can damage tails, feet, and shell edges.

## Feeding Competition

Tank mates may compete for food. One turtle may eat too much while another receives too little.

## Biting And Territorial Stress

Turtles may bite, chase, block basking access, or stress each other. Stress can reduce feeding and activity.

## Quarantine

New turtles should be quarantined before any shared environment is considered. This helps reduce disease and parasite risk.

## Separation Plans

Keepers should prepare a separate tank or container before problems happen. Separation is often the safest option.
