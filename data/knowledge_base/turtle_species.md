# Turtle Species

## Taiwan Stripe-necked Turtle

Taiwan stripe-necked turtles are semi-aquatic turtles that need both swimming space and a completely dry basking area. They are active, alert, and usually benefit from a clean tank with stable access to land.

- Tank needs: swimming area, dry basking platform, gentle ramp, strong filtration.
- Diet notes: quality turtle pellets, aquatic plants or vegetables, and occasional animal protein depending on age.
- Basking needs: UVB light and a heat lamp over the dry platform.
- Compatibility notes: avoid mixed-species housing when size, diet, or behavior differs.
- Common risks: poor water quality, no basking area, lack of UVB, and escape attempts.

## Red-eared Slider

Red-eared sliders are hardy but produce heavy waste and grow larger than many beginners expect. They need a spacious aquarium, strong filtration, and daily access to basking.

- Tank needs: large water volume, strong filter, stable dry basking dock.
- Diet notes: juveniles eat more protein; adults need more plant matter.
- Basking needs: UVB and heat are important for shell and calcium health.
- Compatibility notes: sliders may bite or compete for food and basking space.
- Common risks: overfeeding, dirty water, shell problems, and aggression.

## African Side-necked Turtle

African side-necked turtles are mostly aquatic but still need safe access to rest and bask. They may be shy and should be kept in stable, low-stress environments.

- Tank needs: warm water, hiding spaces, resting areas, gentle access to air.
- Diet notes: omnivorous diet with pellets and suitable protein sources.
- Basking needs: provide a dry basking area even if the turtle uses it less often.
- Compatibility notes: avoid housing with aggressive or much larger turtles.
- Common risks: stress, poor water temperature, and insufficient resting access.

## Musk Turtle

Musk turtles are smaller and often prefer shallower water with many resting points. They are not strong open-water swimmers compared with some larger aquatic turtles.

- Tank needs: moderate water depth, climbing surfaces, hides, and easy access to air.
- Diet notes: sinking pellets and suitable protein foods are commonly used.
- Basking needs: UVB and a dry basking option should still be available.
- Compatibility notes: avoid housing with large turtles that may bully or injure them.
- Common risks: water too deep without rest areas, poor water quality, and bites from tank mates.

## Map Turtle

Map turtles are active aquatic turtles that often need excellent water quality and good basking access. They can be sensitive to dirty water.

- Tank needs: swimming space, strong filtration, basking area, and clean water.
- Diet notes: quality pellets, aquatic foods, and species-appropriate variety.
- Basking needs: reliable basking with UVB and heat is important.
- Compatibility notes: avoid crowded tanks and mixed housing with aggressive species.
- Common risks: shell issues, stress, and water quality problems.
